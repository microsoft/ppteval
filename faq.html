What domain does PPT-Eval benchmark?

GUI-based slide editing and creation in PowerPoint Online—requiring multimodal understanding and precise manipulation beyond static text generation.

How many tasks are included?

120 tasks across 12 files, organized into three difficulty tiers: Easy (51), Medium (39), and Hard (30).

Why use rubric scoring?

Tasks often admit multiple valid solutions and agents make partial progress; binary success/failure metrics fail to capture meaningful distinctions. Our rubrics award fractional credit and give natural-language feedback, achieving a Kendall's τb of 0.77 with human judgments.

What metrics should I focus on?

Success Rate (SR, perfect completions) and Average Score (mean rubric score) jointly reveal capability and incremental progress. For example, Claude-4.5-Opus achieves SR 45% / Avg. Score 57%.

How are scores aggregated?

A modified formula balances progress on critical checkpoints with a penalty factor (λ=0.3) from non-critical issues—avoiding harsh zeroing of near-complete attempts while preserving the importance of essential changes.

What models were evaluated?

Closed models: Claude-4.5-Opus, Claude-4-Sonnet, and OpenAI's Computer-Use-Preview. Open-weights: OpenCUA (7B & 32B) and Qwen3-VL-Instruct (8B & 32B). All models use a maximum budget of 30 steps per task.

What are common failure modes?

Misplaced objects, incomplete multi-element diagram updates, unintended animations, and destructive global changes.
